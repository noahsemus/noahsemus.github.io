Ambient CIT

Ambient CIT

Ambient Computationally Intelligent Technology

Please Note:
This project is still being worked on.

Ambient Compuationally Intelligent Technology is my senior thesis within the Communications Design Program at Pratt. This idea takes a speculative look at the future and how technology integrates into our lives. When does it become so closely a part of us as a species that it reconnects into the human experience/ethos? This is the question that I am hoping to answer. The key aspect of ACIT lies within the human part of it. The idea is perhaps less about the techonology itself, but rather how do we, as humans, change and interact with ambiently intelligent computational devices?

The document shown here is the initial research document created around the thesis.

Stage 1: Wayfinding

The beginning of the exploration starts here. Using ACIT as a frame of looking at the world, I attempted to understand how wayfinding would work.

I ultimately landed on an auditory experience. One of the biggest frustrations people have with wayfinding is the visual aspect of it. It can be incredibly hard to deconstruct signage and other visuals if not expertly crafted. However, if an ambient auditory beacon was created, there would be no frustration. The beacon would be conformed to each individual person based on their needs, wants, and personality. The sounds created by such beacon would not be intrusive or loud, but rather simply there, guiding the person to each point in which they need to go.

Stage 2: Newspaper

Visit the live site! (Desktop Only)

All deliverables were created and/or coded by me.

The second part of the exploration lives here. How does the frontpage of the New York Times change in an ambient world? In juxtaposition to the previous stage, here the world envisioned is perhaps far more grounded. Rather than being clean and warm, the world is grimey, imperfect, and ultimately more real.

In order to design the frontpage of the newspaper, I thought about how we get the newspaper in the first place. Normally, we go up to one of the many newspaper machines sprinkled around NYC and purchase one of them there. From this, I envisioned how those machines would evolve as well, as I thought them to be just as important as the frontpage itself.

The machine identifies who is approaching it, and using data collected from the cloud, will rearrange the frontpage based on that user's needs. If that user wants to know more about politics, that is what they'll see. Sports? Then they'll see that. Ultimately, however, it is all the same content -- it is simply the layout heirarchy that is being changed.

The digital screen and print editions are both designed on separate modular grids.

Stage 3: Interface

All deliverables were designed, modeled, and animated by me.

The final part of the trilogy culminates in more grandiose overview in a similar frame to the first project. What does the idea of ambience look like in a larger context than a single object?

To show this, I created what I dubbed the "the Ambient Lifestyle". Ultimately this was meant to show the interconnectedness and recession of technology in our lives.
